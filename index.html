My Work

Experience

I am currently working in Prof. Dawn Song’s lab at UC Berkeley on deep learning research. I'm focusing on improving neural network performance under difficult conditions; e.g. adversarial examples, corrupted data, data with unwanted spurious correlations, etc.

Summer 2019, worked as a software engineering intern at Okta in SF. I was on the Core Infrastructure Reliability Team, where I worked on a new, scalable application monitoring system that solved problems that external monitoring solutions could not.

The Summer and Fall of 2018, I worked at Remeeting, a startup in Berkeley that develops state-of-the-art automatic speech recognition (ASR) technology. I mainly worked on a WebRTC-based video conferencing product from scratch that integrates Remeeting’s ASR. I also learned a lot about speech recognition, trained a few HMM-GMM/DNN acoustic models on large datasets using Kaldi, and built a GMM-based language-ID tool.

In Spring and Fall 2017, I was involved with Codebase@Berkeley, a student-run organization that provides SWE services to high-growth startups. I led an 8 person team on a semester-long full-stack development project, where we delivered prototype AWS backend for a local Berkeley startup.
